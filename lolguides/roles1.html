Top Laner, Jungler and Mid Laner Breakdown

Learn more about the solo laners in League of Legends. The roles where you do not depend as much on others.

Top Laner

The top laner in League of Legends typically plays in the top lane, which is the lane that is farthest away from the Dragon and Baron pit. The role of the top laner is to provide a strong front line for their team, absorb damage, and disrupt enemy champions.

Top laners often play champions that are durable, such as tanks or bruisers, who can survive damage and control team fights with their abilities. They are responsible for holding their own lane, pushing enemy minions, and taking down enemy turrets.

Jungler

The jungler is responsible for controlling the neutral monsters in the jungle and helping their team secure objectives such as Dragon and Baron Nashor. In addition, the jungler's role involves ganking, which means attacking enemy champions in their lanes to help their team secure kills or objectives.

The jungler is a key player which can greatly influence the outcome of the match by properly controlling the map and helping every player. Junglers often play champions which have a crowd-control ability (CC), this type of abilities lock down enemy champions and makes them easy to kill.

Mid Laner

The role of the mid laner is to provide high damage output, control the game through their abilities, and roam to other lanes to help secure kills or objectives. As this role plays in the middle of the map, which means that they have easy access to every other lane.

The mid laner is often a champion with high damage output, such as mages or assassins. They need to use their abilities to deal damage to the enemy team and control the game.
